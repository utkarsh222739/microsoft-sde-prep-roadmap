The Loop

How the Microsoft interview works

Before diving into topics, know the shape of the funnel. Most IDC loops follow this sequence; the depth of each stage scales with seniority. Exact rounds vary by team and role.

1

Online Assessment (OA)

2–3 timed DSA problems on Codility / HackerRank. Common for SDE 1 & campus hires. Optimize for correctness + edge cases under time pressure.

2

Technical Phone / Recruiter Screen

One coding problem on a shared editor plus a resume walkthrough. Talk through your approach out loud — communication is scored.

3

The Loop — 4–5 rounds (usually virtual)

Historically an on-site visit, the loop is now most often run remotely over Microsoft Teams (in-person still happens for some teams). Mix of coding + design rounds, 45–60 min each, each probing a different competency.

4

“As-Appropriate” (AA) Round

A senior leader / hiring manager makes the final call — only scheduled if your earlier rounds go well. Heavier on behavioral signal, ambiguity handling, and culture fit.

5

Debrief & Decision

Interviewers sync, level you, and align on the offer. Consistent signal across rounds matters more than one standout answer.

🚀 Domain expertise & ownership

Interviewers go deep on your past projects. Being able to articulate the architecture of complex systems you’ve built — e.g. orchestrating autonomous AI agents using the Model Context Protocol (MCP) — is a strong signal of technical depth and ownership. Prepare a crisp architecture narrative: the problem, constraints, your design choices, the trade-offs you rejected, and the measurable impact.

Microsoft culture & behavioral

Microsoft weights behavioral rounds heavily to assess alignment with its core values — above all, a Growth Mindset: learning from failure, collaborating across teams, and adapting under pressure.

⭐ The STAR Method

Prepare 6–8 structured stories you can flex across questions. Lead with the result, quantify the impact.
